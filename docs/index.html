The idea

You don't learn music from a textbook. You learn it by sitting at a piano, playing something badly, listening to what went wrong, and trying again. AI Jam Sessions gives an LLM the same experience ‚Äî real audio, real feedback, real learning.

Five sound engines + layered combinator

From zero-dependency additive synthesis to 480-sample Salamander Grand to physical vocal tract modeling. Layer any two together.

EngineTypeSound
Oscillator Piano Additive synthesis Hammer noise, inharmonicity, 48-voice polyphony, stereo imaging. Zero dependencies.
Sample Piano WAV playback Salamander Grand ‚Äî 480 samples, 16 velocity layers, 88 keys.
Vocal (Sample) Pitch-shifted samples Sustained vowel tones with portamento and legato mode.
Vocal Tract Physical model Pink Trombone ‚Äî LF glottal waveform, 44-cell waveguide. Soprano, alto, tenor, bass.
Vocal Synth Additive synthesis 15 Kokoro voice presets. Formant shaping, breathiness, vibrato. Deterministic.
Layered Combinator Wraps two engines ‚Äî piano+synth, vocal+synth, any combination.

The browser cockpit

A full instrument and vocal studio in your browser. No plugins, no DAW ‚Äî just a web page with a piano.

  • Dual-mode piano roll ‚Äî instrument (chromatic) or vocal (vowel-colored)
  • Visual keyboard ‚Äî QWERTY-mapped, two octaves from C4
  • 20 voice presets ‚Äî Kokoro, tract, choir, synth-vox
  • 10 instrument presets ‚Äî 6 piano voices + synth-pad, organ, bell, strings
  • Note inspector ‚Äî edit velocity, vowel, breathiness per note
  • 7 tuning systems ‚Äî equal, just, Pythagorean, meantone, Werckmeister III, custom
  • Tuning audit ‚Äî frequency table, beat-frequency analysis
  • Score import/export ‚Äî full JSON serialization
  • LLM API ‚Äî window.__cockpit for programmatic composition

Piano roll visualization

The practice journal
