## 1. Research grounding (new findings 34–46; dispatch findings 1–33 remain in force)

Two study-swarm lanes (2026-07-11, retrieval-mandatory, this session): **Lane A** grounding-trace question taxonomy + cross-format transfer; **Lane B** synthetic volume + mix ratios. New findings numbered continuing the dispatch's 1–33. Citation misattribution caught during synthesis and corrected before locking: arXiv:2509.03934 is Huang et al. (SelfAug), confirming the dispatch's finding-6 attribution; the lane's "Wu et al." was a middle-author error.

### Lane A — taxonomy + transfer

34. **APIGen structures synthetic function-calling data into four query families (simple / multiple-function / parallel / parallel-multiple), each example verified by format check → real execution → semantic check (>95% correct on a 600-sample human audit); the resulting 60k set trained a 7B past GPT-4 variants on BFCL.** Liu et al. 2024, APIGen (arXiv:2406.18518). *Implication: our taxonomy spans single-call lookup, aggregation, and multi-call composition; every synthetic item is execution-verified. Extends finding 31.*
35. **8k compositional examples built bottom-up — atomic tasks heuristically composed into chained tasks, then rolled into trajectories — measurably improved compositional multi-turn function calling.** Chen et al. 2024, BUTTON (arXiv:2410.12952). *Implication: include a chained 2-call family built by composing atomic templates; small counts suffice.*
36. **Generalization to unseen instructions emerges only past a diversity threshold ACROSS semantic families — diversifying within one narrow family fails, and diversity beats more-samples-per-template at fixed budget.** Zhang, Wang & Charton 2024, Only-IF (arXiv:2410.04717). *Implication: spend synthesis budget on more question families and parameter regions, not more repeats per template — 9 families × few repeats, not 3 families × many.*
37. **Cross-format skill transfer is real but partial: size-matched MCQ-only SFT raised MCQ accuracy (47.7→53.2) while cross-format consistency stayed flat, and expanding only ~30% of data into multiple formats recovered ~88% of the multi-format gain.** Liu et al. 2026, Multi-Format Training (arXiv:2606.11643). *Implication: vary ANSWER formats inside the free-form envelope (sentence / terse-value / yes-no verification ≈ 65/23/12) — free-form-only in one answer shape risks under-delivering on the MCQ eval; the residual free-form→MCQ transfer gap is this design's stated known risk (§13).*
38. **MCQ evaluation aligns poorly with human grading of the same models' free-form answers; model rankings change between formats.** Chandak et al. 2025 (arXiv:2507.02856). *Implication: same risk as 37, from the eval side; P3-v1 tracks free-form grounding accuracy as the selection signal while P5 remains the sealed MCQ harness — both reported.*
39. **At ~0.7k-example scale, single-turn-only SFT beat multi-turn-only SFT even on multi-turn eval (20.2% vs 15.9%, untrained 22.7%), attributed to noisy accumulated-error trajectories.** Liu et al. 2026 (arXiv:2606.00135). *Implication: per-exchange correctness dominates; our sessions are deterministic-verified (no accumulated error), but we still weight toward short sessions.*
40. **Multi-turn conversations in post-training yielded +12% on multi-turn evaluation; single-turn-only leaves a multi-turn gap.** Graf et al. 2026, TurnWise (arXiv:2603.16759). *Implication (with 39): mix — 60% single-QA sessions, 40% two/three-QA sessions, every exchange independently verified.*
41. **LoRA fine-tuning with format augmentations improved accuracy but improved format ROBUSTNESS in only 1 of 8 models; punctuation-level template variation is not the lever.** Seleznyov et al. 2025 (arXiv:2508.11383). *Implication: paraphrase banks vary STRUCTURAL framing (clause order, question syntax, register), not punctuation; tempers expectations for component 1 — it hardens the trained family, it does not buy robustness for free. Complements finding 25.*
42. **Distribution shift correlates with forgetting severity; on exactly our base (Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct), task-specific SFT dropped IFEval 71.90→45.10 (−26.8), and replay aligned to the model's OWN output distribution mitigates it.** Huang et al. 2025, SelfAug (arXiv:2509.03934; IFEval figures from the paper body, retrieved by the lane). *Implication: the strongest single motivation for component 3 (self-rehearsal); confirms finding 6 on our exact substrate.*

### Lane B — volume + mix

43. **Special-purpose synthetic slices have a sharp optimum near ~10–15% of the mix; overall accuracy degraded monotonically as the slice grew past it.** Lin et al. 2024, Hammer (arXiv:2410.04587). *Implication: cap the yes/no-verification answer sub-family at ~12% of grounding items and the rehearsal component at ~12% of total examples. Extends finding 31.*
44. **Accumulating synthetic data ALONGSIDE retained real data bounds test error independent of iteration count, and accumulation stays stable even as the real fraction trends toward zero; replacing real data collapses.** Gerstgrasser et al. 2024 (arXiv:2404.01413) + Kazdan et al. 2024, Collapse or Thrive (arXiv:2410.16713). *Implication: all 78 human records stay verbatim in every v1 mix (they do — §3); no magic safe-fraction exists, retention + verification is the mechanism. Extends finding 32.*
45. **At low-data scale, mixing task families SYNERGIZES rather than conflicts; total amount influences performance more than composition ratio; general ability plateaus ≈1k samples.** Dong et al., ACL 2024 (arXiv:2310.05492). *Implication: ~500 total examples is the right order; the jam:grounding ratio is second-order — roughly balanced hundreds per family beats ratio-tuning.*
46. **Replay data reconstructed to match the base model's own instruction distribution preserved general benchmarks better than third-party general datasets (their fixed mix 83/17 general/domain; no fraction ablation).** Ding & Wang 2025 (arXiv:2506.09428). *Implication (with 42): rehearsal examples are the base model's OWN greedy outputs on pinned generic prompts — not an external instruct dataset (also avoids provenance/licensing imports into a published dataset's companion artifact).*

**Honest gaps the lanes reported:** no source quantifies a synthetic:human ratio threshold for tool-use SFT; no source gives a minimum surface-variant count per template; no direct study of terse-format-compliance examples preserving format-following; no 100→500→5k example-count curve for a fixed small toolset. Where a number was needed anyway, §3 states which finding anchors it and that the residual is judgment.

**Also in force from v0's own receipts (not web):** epoch selections {2,4,4,4,4} replicated across two GPUs (few-epochs won decisively — this substrate's own evidence, weighted above finding 29's long-CoT result); schema validity 1.000 from epoch 2; exact-match ceiling caused by unseen-song canonical-id lookup, not format.

## 3. v1 training-set design (the only substantive change vs v0)

Three components, **494 examples** total. System prompt for ALL components: the v0 `SYSTEM_TEXT` byte-identical (`You are operating AI Jam Sessions, a music education platform.`). Every SFT line carries `tools_key ∈ {mcp41, inspector9, none}`; the trainer renders each line with its own catalog (§7 delta 2).

### Component 1 — jam traces, paraphrase-augmented (L10 verbatim) — 234 examples, `tools_key: mcp41`

- The **78 human gradient records verbatim** (findings 32/44 — every human record stays in the mix), plus **2 paraphrase variants per record = 156 synthetic** jam examples.
- **Paraphrase = user-turn substitution ONLY.** Assistant turns (prose + `tool_calls`) and tool-result turns are **byte-frozen** from the human record. Consequence: 100% of component-1 loss-bearing tokens (assistant spans) are human-authored — the collapse surface for this component is structurally zero (finding 44), and the frozen action targets are exactly the L10 requirement.
- Paraphrase bank: ≥24 structural surface templates (finding 41: clause order / question syntax / register variation, not punctuation), slots = {song reference, measure range, focus tail}. The **focus tail** (e.g. "the dominant preparation") is carried from the original human ask verbatim — no invented musical claims. Measure numbers and song reference MUST appear literally in every variant (verifier-checked). Variant choice per (record, k) is LCG-seeded (§5).
- Inner-val records (chopin-prelude-e-minor, fur-elise) are **never paraphrased** — no derivative of a selection record enters the gradient set.

### Component 2 — grounding-shaped traces (the arc-earned amendment) — 200 train sessions, `tools_key: inspector9`

- Multi-turn QA sessions where the assistant answers musical questions about a TRAIN-record phrase by calling the **9-tool inspector surface** (`inspectorToolSchemas()` from `midi-inspector.ts` — the exact catalog the sealed `tool_inspected` condition declares at eval time), then answers grounded in the returned result.
- **Session shape:** user turn = compact context header (song title + phrase window, e.g. "We're looking at Für Elise, measures 1–8.") + a free-form question; assistant turn = brief intent prose + one `tool_call`; tool turn = the REAL executed inspector result (compact `JSON.stringify`, v0 serialization parity); assistant turn = natural-language answer embedding the tool-derived value. 60% of sessions have 1 QA exchange, 25% have 2, 15% have 3 (findings 39/40); ≈310 QA items total.
- **NO annotation prose in grounding traces** — 15 of 16 cohort records are train records whose `annotation_target` text appears verbatim inside the sealed eval's user prompts; excluding annotations from training keeps the contamination story airtight (§4). The "context lacks the fact → call the tool" skill is preserved without it.
- **Question taxonomy — 9 families, independently authored** (finding 36: breadth over repeats; finding 34: lookup/aggregation/composition coverage). Every family's parameters are sampled from the record's ACTUAL MIDI (LCG-seeded); the gold value is computed by executing the real inspector function; families are phrased in ≥4 structural surface variants each:
  - F1 position lookup → `get_pitch_at` (what sounds at measure M, beat B, hand H)
  - F2 extremes → `find_highest_pitch` / `find_lowest_pitch` (optionally hand-restricted)
  - F3 texture/balance → `get_hand_balance` (which hand carries more; how the notes split)
  - F4 pitch-class census → `count_notes_with_pitch_class` / `count_distinct_pitch_classes`
  - F5 downbeat rhythm → `count_beat_1_onsets`
  - F6 measure contents → `get_events_in_measure` (what happens in m. M; note count in m. M)
  - F7 hand contents → `get_events_in_hand` (register span / first-last note of one hand)
  - F8 composition, 2 calls (finding 35) → e.g. busier-measure comparison (`get_events_in_measure`×2), phrase span (`find_highest_pitch`+`find_lowest_pitch`)
  - F9 claim verification → user states a checkable claim ("the left hand is busier here, right?"), assistant verifies via the appropriate tool and confirms/corrects
- **Answer-format mix inside the free-form envelope** (finding 37; cap per finding 43): ≈65% full-sentence answers, ≈23% terse-value compliance ("Answer with just the number." → "14."), ≈12% yes/no verification (F9). **No option lists of any kind, no letter answers** — the kickoff's leakage rule (free-form QA, not MCQ) binds; the residual free-form→MCQ transfer gap is acknowledged in §13.
- **Record coverage:** sessions distributed round-robin over the 78 gradient records (≈2–3 sessions each), so grounding coverage spans all 5 gradient songs incl. every cohort train song's phrases — grounded in TRAIN-record MIDI per the kickoff, with the question/format independence carrying the leakage burden (§4).

### Component 3 — base-distribution self-rehearsal — 60 examples, `tools_key: none` ⚠ NAMED DECISION (§13)

- 60 single-turn generic-instruction examples: a pinned, self-authored bank of 60 prompts (explain / summarize / rewrite / compare / plan / answer, music-free and jam-free topics), completed by **local `qwen2.5:7b` (digest pinned) at temperature 0, greedy, one shot, no retries** — the base model's own distribution, per findings 42/46. 12% of total examples (cap per finding 43).
- Purpose: directly attack the forgetting mechanism the v0 `text_only` −0.119 exposed (finding 42 measured −26.8 IFEval on this exact base without replay). This is the one lock-argued addition beyond the kickoff's two named components — surfaced explicitly at the priced-ask gate; striking it is a one-line rebuild (§13).
- Rehearsal examples are never paraphrased, never in selection, and pass the same contamination gate (trivially — the prompt bank contains no music content).

## 4. Leakage rule + contamination gate (P1-v1 gate G5, mechanical)

**The threat (kickoff, load-bearing):** 15 of the 16 sealed-cohort records ARE train records, and the E3 harness generates its MCQs deterministically from them. Skill-level overlap with the eval is the POINT of this pass; string-level and format-level overlap with the harness is the forbidden thing.

Mechanically asserted by the gate (exit 1 on any hit; normalization = lowercase + whitespace-collapse both sides):

1. **MCQ text blacklist:** `generateQuestionSet()` is run over **all 115 records** (not just the 16 cohort records — stronger than the kickoff's minimum), collecting every computable `questionText` (802 strings, derivation verified this session). No message of any synthetic example may contain any blacklisted string as a substring.
2. **Harness-format markers:** no synthetic message may contain the option-block pattern (regex over `A) … B) … C) … D)`), the strings `Options:`, `respond with ONLY`, `single letter`, `A, B, C, or D`, or any sentence of `E3_TOOL_SYSTEM_TEXT` / the E3 user-prompt scaffold (`Use the MIDI inspector tools as needed`, `Annotation:` header form).
3. **clair-de-lune zero-reference:** the substring `clair` (case-insensitive) appears nowhere in any v1 training or validation file; no record id from the test split is referenced by any synthetic example. (v0 G1 split-integrity asserts also rerun.)
4. **Annotation-prose exclusion:** no `annotation_target` field text from ANY record appears in any component-2 message (asserted by substring over the structure/key_moments/teaching_notes/teaching_goals/style_tips strings of all 115 records).
5. **Provenance boundary:** every component-2 session's `record_ref` ∈ the 78 gradient records (train file) or the 25 inner-val records (val file only); every component-1 paraphrase's base ∈ the 78.

The gate report enumerates blacklist size, per-assert counts, and the shas of every file checked.

## 6. Verifier spec (EXTERNAL_VERIFIER — the tool is the verifier)

- **G6a jam execution verification (APIGen finding 31/34, upgraded from v0's schema-only G2):** every UNIQUE frozen `tool_call` in the train+val jam files (dedup by `(name, canonical-JSON(arguments))`) is executed against the REAL MCP server (`dist/mcp-server.js` over MCP-stdio, `Client`+`StdioClientTransport`, HOME isolated — the repo's own test pattern; proven in this session): `view_piano_roll` must return `isError:false`, `play_song` must return `isError:false` (followed by `stop_playback` hygiene), any error fails the gate. v0's G2 (`validateTrace` AJV, zero mismatches) also reruns on every jam line.

## 8. Inner-validation split + P3-v1 checkpoint selection

- **Songs unchanged (kickoff mandate, comparability):** `chopin-prelude-e-minor` (12) + `fur-elise` (13) held out of the gradient set; the 25 jam inner-val records byte-identical to v0.
- **NEW: grounding-val** — 50 sessions generated from the 25 inner-val records by the same generator with a disjoint seeded stream (§5). Selection-only material from selection-only songs: measures the new skill without touching the gradient set, the cohort, or the harness. Rehearsal has no val component and is never selected on.
- **Per (seed, checkpoint ∈ {2,4,8}) scoring, teacher-forced greedy (v0 §6 machinery):**
  - `jam_exact` + `jam_validity` — v0's two metrics on the 25 jam-val records, unchanged.
  - `g_call` — on each grounding call-turn (context = gold turns before it): fraction where ≥1 `<tool_call>` is emitted AND every emitted call is schema-valid against the inspector catalog (AJV-hardened, v0 parity). Tool-name exact-match is NOT required (alternate valid strategies exist; finding 38 — score the answer, not the path).
  - `g_ans` — on each grounding answer-turn (context includes the gold tool result): fraction passing the §6 containment matcher.
  - `grounding_score = (g_call + g_ans) / 2`.
- **Selection rule (delta vs v0, argued):** highest `(jam_exact + grounding_score) / 2`; tie → higher `jam_validity`; tie → fewer epochs. **Why the change:** v0's jam-only selection was blind to the surface the sealed eval measures and could not see the degradation it later exposed; v1 trains two families and must select on both. The rule stays deterministic, preregistered, and never touches clair-de-lune or the cohort.
- clair-de-lune: untouched by anything in P1–P4 (gate-asserted).
